What the skill does

It creates the stimuli you can show, test, or simulate.

01

Target

Identify the thing that must exist in the world: profile, offer, page, post, price, product, pitch, or script.

02

Family

Group candidates by the versions that would create different human reactions, not by abstract strategy labels.

03

Write

Draft the actual buyer-facing or audience-facing artifacts under every meaningful family.

04

Probe

Name the reaction each artifact needs: trust, desire, suspicion, willingness to pay, confusion, or avoidance.

Example output

The deliverable is a pack of artifacts.

A good run gives you concrete candidates that can be copied, shown to people, put into an experiment, or passed into a later simulation.

Profile family

Buyer-facing

“I help Japanese companies turn AI search into qualified pipeline. Strategy, implementation, and measurement in one sprint.”

Reaction to watch: clear buyer fit, trust, willingness to ask for details.

Offer family

Cash-near service

“AI Search Revenue Sprint: 14 days, 5 rewritten pages, query map, dashboard, and implementation calls. Fixed price: $8,000.”

Reaction to watch: price resistance, perceived specificity, urgency.

Launch family

Direct post

“I’m opening 3 slots for founders entering Japan who need AI-search-native GTM, not translated marketing. DM ‘Japan’ and I’ll send the audit format.”

Reaction to watch: inbound quality, skepticism, shares, silent saves.

Use cases

Built for decisions where the missing artifact is expensive.

Use it when you need the thing itself, not commentary about the thing. The output should be ready to paste into a post, a sales page, an outreach message, a profile, a deck, or a later simulation run.

01

“What should I build next?” becomes named product concepts, buyer promises, first screens, offers, and no-build tests.

02

“How should I launch this?” becomes actual launch posts, DM scripts, waitlist copy, discount terms, and partner pitches.

03

“What should my profile say?” becomes profile drafts a stranger can judge, not advice about sounding credible.
